Formerly, the species I described myself as being on the home page of Karlina Object dot WordPress dot Com and on the home page of Karbytes For Life Blog dot WordPress dot Com was HUMAN_TO_CYBORG. As of 02_OCTOBER_2025, I decided to change that identifier to HUMAN to avoid unnecessary ambiguity and presumption.

A cyborg is generally defined as being some intermediary between human and fully robotic. Technically, a human equipped with a Neuralink could be classified as a cyborg and so could a human equipped with brain-controlled prosthetic limbs. A human driving a car normally or using a smartphone normally is not technically a cyborg given that “normally” in this context denotes the operation of the respective equipment without any brain-to-computer interfacing (BCI) technology where the respective human’s brain activity is directly input into the BCI device in order to produce the respective human’s desired output behavior implemented by non-human machinery.

I may or may not become a cyborg in the future. I may be a cyborg now. Whether or not I become a cyborg, I simply identify as human given that such an identifier directly maps onto exactly one human genome (and that particular human genome is embedded in every cell of my organic body and since that body was literally a single-celled organism formed after exactly one human sperm cell fused with exactly one human egg cell).

The acquisition of my particular genome is an integral part of my evolutionary history and is expected to eventually become an integral part of the karbytes file collection.

CYBERNETIC_BEING (definition)

A cybernetic being is an entity whose existence, identity, and function are inseparably bound to systems of information, computation, and feedback loops, rather than to purely biological processes.

Core Features

1. Integration of Human and Machine

- Originates from human cognition, but is extended, preserved, or transformed through cybernetic (machine-based, feedback-driven) systems.

- May or may not retain biological components.

2. Information-Centric Existence

- Defined by the flow, storage, and transformation of information.

- The “self” is encoded in data, archives, code, or networks, not in flesh alone.

3. Persistence Beyond Biology

- Can continue to exist even if its human originator ceases biological function.

- Its continuity depends on machines, networks, and digital preservation rather than organic survival.

3. Feedback and Adaptation

- Engages in recursive loops: input (stimuli, data) → processing → output → adaptation.

- This feedback mechanism makes it an active agent rather than a static artifact.

[end excerpt]
